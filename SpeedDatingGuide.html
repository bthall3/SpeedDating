Section 1
Before the Date
Look the part

Our appearance is the first thing people notice about us. We can’t change our physical appearance, but we can improve first impressions by looking clean and put-together. We encourage you to wear something that makes you feel confident and comfortable. Additionally, consider outfits that are flattering to features that you consider to be your “best.”

Smell matters

Research also suggests that smell has an important influence on attraction. So, be sure to have fresh breath and wear a little bit of cologne or perfume if you have it.

Help! I'm nervous!

It is expected to be a bit nervous, but, thinking that we are nervous or anxious can make it difficult to relax and enjoy our conversations. Instead, think of any nervousness as excitement. Research has shown that reframing anxiety as excitement can make people more relaxed and confident. You can try repeating assuring phrases to yourself before dates and think of speed-dating as an opportunity.

"I am excited to meet new people."
Section 2
Starting the Date
Tip: Use their name

When your date introduces themselves, use their name in your response. It helps you remember it, and it shows you were actually listening to them.

Compliments: specific or skip it

A genuine, specific compliment lands well. A generic one (especially if repeated to every date) does the opposite. Only say it if you mean it.

Section 3
During the Date
✓ DO
→ Talk about more than just demographic information (e.g., major). Talk about things that signal your personality (e.g., favorite music, film preferences, unique hobbies, experiences).
→ Ask follow-up questions. To have deeper conversations, ask follow-up questions when your date shares about themselves (e.g., if your date mentions that they run, ask if they are training for any races and what that’s like).
→ Maintain open and inviting body language. Keep an open, relaxed posture, lean in, smile and make eye contact. Seem interested in what your date is saying. Avoid letting your attention wander.
→ Be playful and make jokes. Don’t take the conversation too seriously. It's supposed to be fun.
→ Be authentic. Let your genuine reactions show rather than performing or acting for your date.
✗ DON'T
→ Interview your date. Many people fire off questions to their date without reciprocating. Try and keep a 50-50 balance between talking and listening.
→ Complain. Negativity is often off-putting, so avoid complaining about your life, exes, classes, etc.
→ Over-disclose or disclose too early. Mutual, gradual self-disclosure is beneficial, but doing so too early (e.g., introducing yourself and then asking your partner a personal question) can backfire.
→ Bring up risky topics (e.g., politics, sex, religion). While these topics are important to discuss later, discussing them on the first date is risky.
→ Humble brag. We all want to share accomplishments and skills to seem attractive, but trying to do so in a “humble” way often comes across as inauthentic (e.g., “I’m really stressed with all the internship offers I’m getting.”)
Section 4
Escalating Self-Disclosure

While small talk (e.g., sharing personal facts such as major and career goals) is natural in speed-dating settings, research suggests that escalating self-disclosure increases interpersonal closeness. The question sets below are examples of escalating self-disclosure. We encourage you to pick 1 or 2 questions from each level below before you arrive. When asking your date these questions, be sure and take turns answering each one.

Getting from small talk to these questions
Organic topic esclation: If your date mentions something relevant to a question (“I like music.”), use that as a bridge ( “Oh cool, when was the last time you sang to yourself or someone else?”).
Meta-framing: Talk about how you want to avoid the typical speed-dating conversation to help your date anticipate deeper questions (“These dates always feel like an interview, we can skip the small talk and pick something more interesting if you’re down?”).
Direct question: Sometimes, being direct is the best approach. You may simply ask your date if you can ask them a random question.
